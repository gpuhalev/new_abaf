Romanian athletes are the Balkan leaders for youth

14 countries took part in the Balkan Youth Championships in Sliven with more than 450 athletes competing. The best ones were coming from Romania.



The athletes representing Romania took total 26 medals – 11 gold, 8 silver and 7 bronze. The second team in the Medals table was the host team of Bulgaria with 2 gold, 2 silver and 3 bronze (total 7 medals). The one of the newest country members of ABAF – Cyprus won 3 medals (2 gold and 1 bronze) for the third place in this statistic.



Romania was the absolute leader in the points as wekk – 286 points against 137.5 points for the second team Croatia and 135.5 points for Greece at the third place.

Bitan Gabriel )ROU) won the boys' long jump with 7,25, while his temammate. Anitei Georgiana was number 1 in the girls' triple jump with 13.34. Toader Andrei Raraes won the boys' shot put (5 kg) with 21.25 m.

The fastest sprinters were Haris Musovic (BIH) and Ioana Teodora Gheorghe (ROU). Musovic won the first place in 100 m boys final in 11.09 (10.83 in semi-final) Gheorghe made 100 m girls' final 12.05 (11.89 in semi-final).



"This event has an utmost importance for the athletics movement, because the youth athletes are the future of the sport. I wish good luck to all Balkan athletes during the hole year", said Balkan Athletics President Dobromir Karamarinov.
