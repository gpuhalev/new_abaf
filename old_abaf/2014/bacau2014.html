Romanian Oprea and Rotaru are the Number 1 athletes at the Balkan championships




Romanian athletes Marian Oprea and Alina Rotaru achieved the best results according to the IAAF scoring table during the Balkan championships in Pitesti (Romania) and earned 2400 euros each from the prize money of 20 000 euros total.


In his hometown Opera won the triple jump men with 16.77. He made a golden double in the competition, because he was Number 1 in the long jump as well with 7.78.


Rotaru achieved 6.72 m in the women long jump.

Romania won the most medals during the weekend – 32 total (14 gold, 12 silver and 6 bronze). The Turkish athletes collected 21 medals (7+7+7). Bulgarian team got 16 medals (7+6+3).


Athletes from 15 countries competed in the Balkan championships, which took place at the renovated stadium "Nikolae Dobrin" in Pitesti.


Two European Athletics Vice presidents - Jean Gracia (FRA) and Karel Pilny (CZE) were among the special guests at the competition. Two Olympic Romanian champions – Gabriela Szabo and Yolanda Balash, were at the stadium too. Szabo opened the Balkan championships as a Sports Minister of Romania.
