Balkan Athletics Gala 2013, a brilliant celebration of a successful year, took place in Yerevan on 29 October.



The best Balkan athletes for 2013 were awarded in Yerevan during a glamorous ceremony in the Armenian capital.


The male rising Stars are Matija Greguric of Croatia who took the gold medal in hammer throw at the Wordl Youth Championships in Donetsk and Mesud Pezer of Bosnia and Herzegovina who won the European Junior Championships in shot put and is Balkan Junior Champion in shot put and discus throw.


The male Rasing Stars are Matija Greguric of Croatia who took the gold medal in hammer throw at the Wordl Youth Championships in Donetsk and Mesud Pezer of Bosnia and Herzegovina who won the European Junior Championships in shot put and is Balkan Junior Champion in shot put and discus throw.


Balkan Athletes of the Year 2013 are Ivana Spanovic, and Emir Bekric, both from Serbia. Spanovic took the bronze medal and set a national record of 6.82 m in long jump at the World Championship in Moscow. She is also Balkan Senior Champion and successfully competed for the Balkan team at the DecaNation meeting earlier this year. The award was given to her by the Minister of Youth and Sport Yury Vardanyan – a true legend in sport himself.


Bekric took the bronze at 400m Hurdles from the World Championships in Moscow with a national record, the gold from the European Junior Championships in Rieti and, the gold from the Balkan Championships in Stara Zagora.


Sergey Bubka, IAAF Vice president, Jean Gracia, European Athletics Vice President, Ahmed Al Kamali, IAAF Council Member and President of the Gulf Countries Athletics organization as well as lot of Ministers and local dignitaries attended the Gala in Yerevan to enjoy a beautiful celebration of athletics. The hosts from Armenian Athletic Federation and personally Robert Emmiyan had done a wonderful job in organising the ceremony where the rich Arminian culture was presented by a variety of songs and dances and by providing excellent conditions for the Balkan federation representatives.


Earlier the same day the Balkan delegation had a meeting in the Armenian Ministry of Youth and Sports of Armenia where the Minister Yury Vardanyan offered a medal to President Dobromir Karamarinov for his evident contribution for the promotion of athletics in the region. The delegation was then received by the President of the Republic of Armenia Mr. Serzh Sargsyan who expressed his content of Armenian federation being part of the Balkan Athletics. He considered the problems and challenges facing the sport in the country and promised his full support for the athletics federation. The last important signature was also laid down on the documents for the reconstruction of the athletics stadium in Yerevan.


The delegation also paid respect to the Memorial for the victims of Armenian genocide Tsitsernakaberd.
